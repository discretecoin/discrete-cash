The Threat

When the curve breaks,
everything breaks.

Harvest-now-decrypt-later is only the opening move. Nearly every coin in circulation rests its ownership and its emission on elliptic-curve signatures. The day a cryptographically-relevant quantum computer arrives, those signatures can be forged at will — not merely archived data decrypted. Balances can be seized, coinbase rewards counterfeited, and the integrity of issuance itself collapses across an entire ECC chain, all at once.

Today's chains

Ownership and emission, forgeable

ECDSA and EdDSA decide who owns what — and who may mint new supply. Shor's algorithm recovers private keys from the public keys exposed on-chain forever, letting an adversary forge signatures, drain existing balances, and counterfeit the coinbase. The whole monetary system fails, not just its confidentiality.

Discrete

Quantum-resistant from genesis

No elliptic curves to break. Discrete signs ownership and authorizes emission with post-quantum cryptography from the first block — so neither your coins nor the supply can be forged, today or after a quantum adversary arrives.

Mechanics

Ownership you can't unwind.
Privacy built from the base layer.

01

Post-quantum ownership

Accounts are anchored to post-quantum signatures rather than elliptic curves. Your coins remain provably yours even against an adversary holding a quantum computer.

02

Short account IDs

Register a short alias on-chain and share it instead of a very long address. Payments still fan out to one-time stealth outputs, so a memorable identifier never costs you receiving privacy.

03

One CPU, one vote

Proof-of-work tuned for commodity CPUs and built to resist ASICs, pools, and botnet farms — a non-outsourceable design that keeps issuance with solo miners rather than a few industrial operators.
